Conduction, Convection, and Radiation

Conduction

Particles in solids vibrate more when heated, and they pass
their vibrations to neighboring particles spreading heat
metals are good conductors because they have a sea delocalised electrons
which carry the K.E through the metal



Convection

Transfer of heat in a fluid is by the movement of the fluid itslef


  1. When heated, the liquid expands, becomes less dense, and rises
  2. The water begins to cool, and is pushed away by rising hot water
  3. As water cools, the liquid contracts, becomes more dense, and sinks
  4. Cool water moves to take place of thr rising water


Radiation

Transfer of heat is by infrared Radiation, which is part of the
electro magnetic spectrum, which can traverse a vaccum

  1. Matte black surfaces are the best emitters and absorbers of radiation
  2. Silver surfaces are the worst emitters and absorbers of radiation
