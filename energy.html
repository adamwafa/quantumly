Energy

An object may have energy due to its motion (K.E) or Position (potential)

Measured in (J) Joules


Types of Energy

  • Kinetic - from movement
    K.E = 1/2 x m x v2

  • Gravitational Potenntial - due to position
    G.P.E = m x g x h

  • Chemical - stored energy between particles

  • Strain - stored energy when an object changes shape

  • Nuclear - stored energy realeased by nuclear reactions

  • total K.E & P.E of particles in a body

  • Electrical - energy carried by a currents

  • Light - energy carried by electromagnetic radiation

  • Sound - energy carried by sound waves


Energy Conservation


Energy can be transferred from one form to another



Energy can be transferred from one object to another



Energy cannot be created or destroyed,
so the total amount of energy before and after a change is the same



Energy Sources

  • Nuclear Fusion (Renewable)

    Hydrogen nuclei with great K.E collide and fuse
    to form helium nuclei

  • Nuclear Fission (Nonrenewable)

    Nuclear energy stored in Uraniam-235 is released by nuclear fission,
    giving out heat which boils water to drive turbine

  • Oil (Nonrenewable)

    Chemical energy is transferred into heat by combustion
    Heat boils water, then steam transfers K.E to turbines,
    which is transformed to elictrical energy through generator

  • Natural Gas (Nonrenewable)

    Chemical energy is transferred into heat by combustion
    Heat boils water, then steam transfers K.E to turbines,
    which is transformed to elictrical energy through generator

  • Coal (Nonrenewable)

    Chemical energy is transferred into heat by combustion
    Heat boils water, then steam transfers K.E to turbines,
    which is transformed to elictrical energy through generator

  • Tidal (Renewable)

    G.P.E of drives turbine (K.E)
    which drives the generator to produce electricity

  • Hydroelectric (Renewable)

    G.P.E of drives turbine (K.E)
    which drives the generator to produce electricity

  • Geothermal (Renewable)

    Water pumped under ground gains heat and boils,
    steam drives turbine to produce electricity

  • Solar (Renewable)

    Light energy is transformed into electricity in a solar cell



Efficiency


Not all energy is useful, some are lost to the surrounding


Efficiency = (Useful energy output / Total input) x 100
