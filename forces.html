Effects of Forces

Forces can change size, shape, speed, or direction of a body



Hooke's Law

The extension of a spring is
directly proportionalto the force applied to it, provided the elastic limit
is not exceeded


Force applied = spring constant (k) x extension (x)



Steps on how to measure the extension of a spring

  1. Set up apparatus-hang spring
  2. Measure the initial length of the spring
  3. Add 100g of mass (1N weight)
  4. Measure new length and subtract intial length to get the extension
  5. Repeat 6 times adding 100g each time
  6. Plot extension against force

Newton's Second Law of Motion

The resultant force acting on an object is proportional to its mass and acceleration

F (N) = m (kg) x a (m/s2)


Resultant Force

The net force when size and direction are taken into acount


When resultant force is zero, forces are equal and opposite

  • Object is at rest
  • Object is travelling at constant speed in a s.t. line


Centripetal Force

an object will move in a curved path when
the force is acting perpendicular to the direction of motion


Speed stays constant, while velocity changes

Object is accelerating towards the center

If the force which provides the centripetal force is seuddenly removed,
the object will leave at a tangent of the circle



Turning effect

The moment of a force is the measure of its turning effect

ex: hinged door, spanner

M (Nm) = F (N) X D (m)


For a body in equilibrium, there is no net moment,
and anticlockwise moment is equal to clockwise moment
