Speed Velocity & Acceleration

Speed (scaler)

Stated as the rate of change in distance

Measured in :
m/sec

Math Relation :
Total distance(m) / total time(s)

Velocity (vector)

Stated as the rate of change in displacement

i.e. speed in a particular direction

Measured in :
m/sec

Math Relation :
Total displacement(m) / total time(s)

Speed.Time Graphs

  1. Horizantal st. line at (0,0) represents body at rest
  2. upward st. line represents body moving with changing speed (Acceleration)
  3. Horizantal st. line at represents body moving with constant speed

Acceleration (VECTOR)

Stated as rate of change in velocity

Measured in :
m/sec2

Math Relation :
Change In Velocity(m/s) / total time(s)

Deceleration is negative Acceleration


Velocity.Time Graphs

  1. a st. line moving upwards uniformly means acceleration is constant

  2. a curved line means accelerration is not constant

    3. Free Fall

  • object falls, speed increases, air resistance increases, then acceleration increases
  • Eventually, air resistance and gravity become equal and opposite, so there is no resultant force
  • No resultant, so no acceleration, speed is constant, Terminal Velocity
